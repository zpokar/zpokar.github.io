Let's Explore Canada

Canada is a country in North America. Its ten provinces and three territories extend from the Atlantic Ocean to the Pacific Ocean and northward into the Arctic Ocean, making it the world's second-largest country by total area, with the world's longest coastline. Its border with the United States is the world's longest international land border. The country is characterized by a wide range of both meteorologic and geological regions. It is a sparsely inhabited country of 40 million people, the vast majority residing south of the 55th parallel in urban areas. Canada's capital is Ottawa and its three largest metropolitan areas are Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver.

Alberta

Alberta is a province in Western Canada. Its landscape encompasses mountains, prairies, desert badlands, and vast coniferous forests. It has more than 600 lakes, and rich mineral deposits. The capital city, Edmonton, is known for the West Edmonton Mall, with shops, and entertainment venues.

Biritish Columbia

British Columbia is a western province in Canada, located between the Pacific Ocean and the Rocky Mountains. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, including lush forests, picturesque coastline, and rugged mountains. Vancouver, the largest city in British Columbia, is a bustling urban center with a diverse population and vibrant arts scene.

Manitoba

Manitoba is a Canadian province bordered by Ontario to the east and Saskatchewan to the west. It's known for its landscape of lakes and rivers. Winnipeg, the capital, is home to the Manitoba Museum, with interactive exhibits on the region's history.

New Brunswick

New Brunswick is one of eastern Canada's Maritime provinces. It encompasses rivers, pine forest, mountains, and the Bay of Fundy, known for extreme tides and whale-watching.

Newfoundland and Labrador

Newfoundland and Labrador is the easternmost province of Canada. It encompasses the island of Newfoundland and mainland Labrador, sharing a border with Quebec. St. John's, the capital, is known for its colorful row houses and historic sites like Signal Hill.

Nova Scotia

Nova Scotia is a Canadian province with large coastal areas, forests, and agricultural lands. Its capital, Halifax, is known for its maritime history.

Ontario

Ontario is a province in east-central Canada that borders the U.S. and the Great Lakes. It's home to Ottawa, Canada's capital, known for Parliament Hill's Victorian architecture and the National Gallery, featuring Canadian and indigenous art. Toronto, Ontario's capital, is home to the CN Tower, with expansive views of the city and Lake Ontario, and the iconic Ontario Science Centre.

Prince Edward Island

Prince Edward Island is one of eastern Canada's maritime provinces, off New Brunswick and Nova Scotia in the Gulf of St. Lawrence. The large island is marked by red-sand beaches, lighthouses, and fertile farmland, and is renowned for seafood like lobster and mussels.

Quebec

Quebec is a predominantly French-speaking province in eastern Canada with a rich history and vibrant culture. Montreal, the largest city in Quebec, is famous for its festivals, diverse culinary scene, and lively arts and music scene.

Saskatchewan

Saskatchewan is a prairie province in Canada, which has a total area of 651,900 square kilometers and a land area of 592,534 square kilometers, the remainder being water area (covered by lakes/ponds, reservoirs and rivers). Saskatchewan is bordered on the west by Alberta, on the north by the Northwest Territories, on the east by Manitoba, and on the south by the U.S. states of Montana and North Dakota.

Northwest Territories

The Northwest Territories is a federal territory of Canada. At a land area of approximately 1,144,000 km2 (442,000 sq mi) and a 2016 census population of 41,786, it is the second-largest and the most populous of the three territories in Northern Canada.

Nunavut

Nunavut is the newest, largest, and northernmost territory of Canada. It was separated officially from the Northwest Territories on April 1, 1999, via the Nunavut Act and the Nunavut Land Claims Agreement Act, though the boundaries had been drawn in 1993.

Yukon

Yukon is the westernmost and smallest of Canada's three territories. It also is the least populated province or territory in Canada, with a population of only 42,154 as reported in the 2016 Census. Whitehorse, the territorial capital and Yukon's only city, is the largest settlement in any of the three territories.
